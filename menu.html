    Shoyu Ramen$10.95
    Shoyu ramen features a clear soy sauce-based broth that is both savory and slightly salty. It is often served with thin, curly ramen noodles and garnished with ingredients like sliced bamboo shoots, spinach, nori seaweed, and a seasoned boiled egg.
    Miso Butter Corn Ramen$13.95
    This ramen variation combines the earthy flavor of miso with the richness of butter and the sweetness of corn. The broth is creamy, and the dish is topped with chashu pork slices, menma (fermented bamboo shoots), and a pat of butter that melts into the soup.
    Tantanmen$11.95
    Tantanmen is known for its spicy and nutty sesame-flavored broth. It's often served with flat wheat noodles and topped with ground pork, chopped bok choy, and green onions. The spiciness level can be adjusted to suit your taste.
    Tsukemen$12.95
    Tsukemen, also known as dipping ramen, consists of thick, chewy noodles served separately from a concentrated broth. Diners dip the noodles into the flavorful broth, which is typically made from pork and soy sauce. It's served with toppings like soft-boiled eggs, nori, and sliced green onions.
    Hakata Ramen$10.95
    Hakata ramen hails from the city of Fukuoka in Japan and is known for its pork bone broth, resulting in a creamy and cloudy soup. The thin, straight noodles are cooked to perfection and served with toppings like pickled ginger, sesame seeds, and thin slices of garlic chashu pork.
